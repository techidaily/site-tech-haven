## How to Overcome Error Code -54 on iPhone: Troubleshooting iTunes Sync Issues After Windows 11 Upgrade

When you try to sync your iPhone with iTunes, you might see an alert message saying that:   
  
**The iPhone cannot be synced.** **An unknown error occurred(-54).**   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/iphone-cannot-be-sync-error-54.png)   
  
This can happen if a file on your computer or iPhone is locked. In most case, you will be able to sync after you click the**OK**button at the bottom of the message.   
  
If the sync still won’t happen, don’t worry, this is an easy question to solve.   
  
Before we begin, please make sure that you have the latest version of iTunes installed on your computer and that your iPhone is already updated to the latest version.   
  
If you are having problem with the recognizing of iPhone to your iTunes, please refer to this post: **[What to do if my iPhone cannot recognize iTunes on Windows 10?](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**   
  
  
1) Click**Start**button, then choose **File Explorer**from the list of choice.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/file-explorer-233x600.png) 

  
2) Head to the**Music** folder.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/music-folder-in-file-explorer.png) 

  
3) Right click**iTunes**folder and choose**Properties**.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/properties-of-itunes-folder.png) 

  
4) Unchecked the**Read Only**option under**Attributes**.  
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/attribute-read-only-untick.png) 

  
5) Click**Apply**and then**OK**to save the change.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/img_57c55ba1ca8a0.png)   
  
 6) You will see a pop-up window saying that the change will be applied to all the sub-folders. Click**OK** to continue.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/confirm-attribute-changes.png) 

  
If the problem still persists, verify that you have full control over the iTunes folder. To do this,  
  
a) Right click**iTunes**folder and choose**Properties**.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/properties-of-itunes-folder.png)   
  
b) Under**Security**tab, click**Edit**button.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/security.png) 

  
c) Make sure the**Full control**option here is allowed. If not, tick the box of**Full control**and hit**Apply**.   
  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/full-control.png) 

  
Once this is done, you should be able to sync your iPhone with your iTunes without problem.
